Terms

Click left or right to see the terms to help you understand how to be a real estate guru

Cash Flow

At a very basic level, this is simply your income minus your expenses

  • If your rental income is $1000 and your expenses are $800 a month, then your cash flow is $200 a month

Cash on Cash Return

Cash on Cash Return (COCR), is the amount of money income minus your expenses

  • If your cash flow is $200 a month, then your annual cash flow is $2400 a year ($240*12 months), assuming you put $20,000, your COCR is $2400/$20,000 or 12%

Cap Rate

In math terms, it is the NOI divided by the value of the property

    If a property is worth $1 million and the NOI is $100,000 a year, the cap rate is 10%

Net Operating Income

Net operating income, also known as NOI is the total income minus total expenses. NOTE: different from cash flow in that it doesn't include mortgage in its calculations

    It is assuming that the house is fully paid off. NOI of a property with $1000 in income and $200 in expenses, the NOI will be $800 a month
